Whatever your reasons for remortgaging, often you can accomplish several of the above goals at once.

You can borrow the money you need to make large purchases or complete costly projects; you can save thousands over the life of the mortgage with a lower interest rate; and you can shorten that life by paying your mortgage off sooner.

Of course, that depends on how much you borrow when you remortgage.

How Do I Qualify?

This isn’t the old days when just about everyone qualified for a remortgage even while borrowing heavily. Financial institutions are now a little more careful about the credit ratings they’ll accept for a remortgage and the amount of money they’ll let you borrow.

To qualify for a remortgage today, you need to be employed, have minimal credit card debt and no judgments against you. We’ll ask you all that up front so you don’t waste your time.

If you do have any of the above, you may still qualify, but it will be critical to fully understand the terms of your mortgage so you don’t end up worse off than when you started.
